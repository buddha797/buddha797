I Want To Hear From You!

Why I'm Running

Student Government at Emory is broken, and I want to help College Council lead the way. College Council manages five hundred thousand dollars of your money, chartering over two hundred clubs, and representing the interests of students in Emory College. College Council is filled with some of the most dedicated and talented people I’ve met at Emory, but the organization isn’t perfect. College Council was founded in 1955, and I will bring it into the 21st century. I will make College Council more professional, more responsive to the student body, and more transparent than any organization on campus. We haven’t given the student body enough of a reason to care about what we do, and its time to change that. As your College Council President, we will lead the way in being a voice for every student in Emory College.

About Me

From my first day at Emory I’ve believed that student government can be reformed to meet the needs of every student. As Speaker of the Senate at Oxford I completely overhauled our documents, created our first code of elections, and wrote our first transparency requirements, and created a system for us to act as a voice for the student body. In Atlanta I led the commission that changed our code of elections after the disastrous 2018 elections, and currently serve as the Chief of Staff for College Council. I have seen firsthand the dysfunctional relationship between student government and the student body, and I believe I have the experience to fix it. Outside of student government, I study Political Science (surprise!) and Computer Science. I want to be a voice for every student in Emory College, so please get in touch!
