Why I'm Running

It’s time to stop settling for a student government that doesn’t represent all students. As president, I will pursue the most ambitious reimagining of College Council since its founding in 1955. As Emory’s largest student governance organization, we will not only restore the trust of the student body, but become a national leader in student representation. As your president, I will make College Council more professional, more responsive to the student body, and more transparent than any organization on campus. We have not given the student body enough of a reason to care about what we do, and it’s time to change that. As your College Council President, we will lead the way in being a voice for every student in Emory College, engaging the entire community to build a better Emory College.

About Me

From my first day at Emory I’ve believed that student government can be reformed to meet the needs of every student. As Speaker of the Senate at Oxford I completely overhauled our documents, created our first code of elections, and wrote our first transparency requirements, and created a system for us to act as a voice for the student body. In Atlanta I led the commission that changed our code of elections after the disastrous 2018 elections, and currently serve as the Chief of Staff for College Council. I have seen firsthand the dysfunctional relationship between student government and the student body, and I believe I have the experience to fix it. Outside of student government, I study Political Science (surprise!) and Computer Science. I want to be a voice for every student in Emory College, so please get in touch!

Transparency

As president, I will make College Council the most transparent organization on campus. With a budget of five hundred thousand dollars, the largest of any student government organization, the student body deserves to know where their money is going. At Oxford I created our first transparency requirements, and this year I passed legislation to open up College Council’s budget to public scrutiny. This legislation also opened up our minutes and other documents. Furthermore, I passed legislation requiring College Council to hold Town Halls at least once per semester to listen to the concerns of the student body and respond to questions in person. I will build on this experience to create and publish an annual budget report so that the student body can see how their money is being spent.

As president, I will hold office hours every week, and make myself available to any student. I will also use Zoom Video Chat to provide access to students who cannot attend in person. Furthermore, I will ensure that College Council has a table every single week at Wonderful Wednesday to listen to the concerns of the student body.

We will create a new website, working with LITS to get a domain name such as collegecouncil.emory.edu. No student government within Emory has been capable of creating a working webpage in years, so clearly a new system is necessary. I will create a Vice President of Technology, who will be responsible for overseeing the webpage, internal communication tools such as Slack, and make sure that College Council is using every tool at our disposal to keep the student body informed. We will use social media and our webpage to publish our minutes every week and livestream all meetings. With all of these tools at our disposal we will make the 64th College Council the most transparent since its creation in 1955. For far too long College Council has not been open to the student body, and rebuilding our relationship starts with complete transparency.

Representation

It is time for College Council to rethink our relationship with student organizations, and work to ensure that the needs every student is represented. College Council should represent every single student in Emory College by expanding the role of student organizations. As president I will restructure College Council, creating a bicameral legislature and expanding student representation. Furthermore, I will reintroduce positions for students intending to enter the Nell Hodgson Woodruff School of Nursing or the Goizueta Business School. With so many students in Emory College planning to enter these schools, it’s time to build these relationships and ensure these students are represented in College Council.

Furthermore, as President I would create a Student Advocacy Center, to provide representation to any student facing conduct violations, honor code violations, academic appeals, or who simply needs help navigating Emory’s bureaucracy. This center will ensure that the rights of every student in Emory College are respected.

Student organizations are not just groups in need of funding, and we should stop thinking of them as such. As many student governments across the nation have done, I will create representation for student groups. From cultural groups to political groups to special interest groups, Emory students come together to create a diverse range of student organizations. These groups represent a diverse student body better than one student government ever could. As president I will create a Student Organization Legislature, comprised of club representatives. This organization will have the power to create and vote on legislation, and provide a powerful way for student organizations to address the concerns of the student body, concerns that may not have been addressed otherwise. This body will help us reimagine College Council, and help us become the most representative organization on campus. Furthermore, in our effort to become more representative, we will reinstate the Greater Emory Committee. First founded in 1911 by the Emory College Student Association, this committee will search for ways to unite Emory College, improve our relationships with other divisional councils, and build One Emory.
